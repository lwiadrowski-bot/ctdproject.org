Neurodivergent Pain Scale

This one goes to 11

"Rate your pain out of 10" assumes we can map what we feel to a number. That requires calibrated interoception, a shared reference frame, and a meaningful zero. None of which are guaranteed for neurodivergent people or those with chronic pain.

This scale replaces "pick a number" with "which description matches?"

How to use: Find the description that matches right now and point to it. The italic notes are for clinicians.
Clinical Rating What I'm experiencing ND Rating
0 No pain My body feels neutral. I'm not aware of any pain.
A genuine zero may be unfamiliar with chronic pain.
 −1
1 Hardly noticeable My pain is barely there. I'm only aware of it if I pay attention.
Low interoception may mean this level isn't registered at all.
 0
2 Noticeable I'm aware of my pain but I can ignore it most of the time.
Alexithymia may make this hard to label , "something's off" rather than "pain."
 1
3 Mild My pain bothers me but I can ignore it most of the time.
Often where the chronic baseline sits. May report "I'm fine" because this is normal.
 2
4 Moderate My pain bothers me. I can still do most activities but I'm aware of it throughout.
May register as fatigue or brain fog rather than "pain."
 3
5 Can't ignore it My pain is always there. Everything takes more effort. I'm starting to adjust around it.
Masking may keep the face neutral. May still say "I'm okay."
 4
6 Dominating attention My pain is the loudest thing in my head. It's making me tired. I'm dropping everything that isn't essential.
May drop suddenly from "fine" to unable. Stimming may increase.
 5
7 Functioning with effort I can do essential things, but each one costs too much. I need to lie down between tasks. Some care activities are no longer doable.
May go nonverbal. Can look like "shutdown," not "in pain."
 6
8 Functioning breaking down I can't think clearly. I can't follow conversation or make decisions. Sleep is wrecked. All activities require pacing.
An ND "7" may look like someone else's "4." Masking persists under extreme pain.
 7
9 Severely limited Getting up, washing, eating feel monumental. Care activities are being sacrificed. Social and work activities can't be tolerated.
May be still and silent. The quiet patient may be in the most pain.
 8
10 Overwhelming Speaking is difficult. I can barely move. Nothing provides relief.
May still answer questions on autopilot. "You seem fine" is not an assessment.
 9
10+ Worst imaginable I can't move from where I am. Nothing touches the pain. I can't ask for help.
Beyond what the standard scale measures.
 10
10++ Beyond the scale I've dissociated. Words have stopped working. I may not remember this afterward.
If this point is reached, it's likely every prior rating was under-reported.
 11
This is a communication tool, not a diagnostic instrument. It's designed to help you describe your experience to a clinician in terms that are more accessible than a numeric scale. It does not replace clinical assessment. If you are in severe pain, seek medical attention.
